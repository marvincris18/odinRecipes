Longanisa

Home

Savor the distinct sweet-savory taste of Longanisa, a beloved Filipino sausage that's perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. This recipe blends ground pork with soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and vinegar, creating a uniquely flavorful sausage. Whether you stuff it into casings or form patties, Longanisa is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. Enjoy it with a side of rice and a tangy vinegar dip or atchara for an authentic Filipino experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar (preferably cane vinegar)
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika (optional, for color)
  • Sausage casings (optional)
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, soy sauce, brown sugar, minced garlic, vinegar, ground black pepper, and paprika (if using). Mix well.
  2. If using sausage casings, stuff the mixture into the casings and tie into individual sausages. Otherwise, form the mixture into small patties or logs.
  3. Heat cooking oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Fry the longanisa until cooked through and browned on all sides.
  5. Serve hot with rice and a side of vinegar or atchara (pickled papaya).
